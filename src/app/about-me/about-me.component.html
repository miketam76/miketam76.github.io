About Me

Documenting the evolution from technical troubleshooting to software architecture.

Hi, I’m Moon (Mike) Tam. My transition into software development is driven by a career-long obsession with how systems work. My foundation was built over several years as an IT Support Technician, where I developed a "troubleshooting-first" mindset. I later scaled this experience during a 2023–2024 student term with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA), working within complex, enterprise-level environments.

Currently, I am further honing my full-stack capabilities through the Web Development & Internet Applications program at Algonquin College. This portfolio is a live record of that progress—showcasing everything from deep-logic Java projects to modern, accessible Angular applications.

When I’m not at my desk, I’m usually on my bike exploring the Ottawa Greenbelt. The quiet focus of those forest trails inspired the theme of this site and provides the mental clarity I need for complex coding. My goal is to leverage this blend of technical intuition and modern engineering to become a Senior Technical Consultant, bridging the gap between sophisticated technology and the people who use it.
